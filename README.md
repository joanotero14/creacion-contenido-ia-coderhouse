The Difference Between Reacting and Choosing

Most decisions feel like choices. But many are simply reactions—to urgency, expectations, habit, or the fear of standing still.

Choosing is different. It requires us to pause, look closer, and ask whether we're moving toward what truly matters, or just continuing because it's familiar.

Experience doesn't always give us more answers. More often, it gives us better questions.

And that's where judgment begins—not in knowing exactly what to do, but in making every decision with greater intention, clarity, and purpose.
